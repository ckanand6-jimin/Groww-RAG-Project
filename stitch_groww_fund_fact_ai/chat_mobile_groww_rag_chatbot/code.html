What is the core difference between a Direct and a Regular Mutual Fund?

10:43 AM

The primary difference lies in how you invest and the fees involved:

Direct Mutual Funds: You invest directly with the AMC (Asset Management Company). Because there are no intermediaries (like brokers or distributors), the expense ratio is lower, which generally leads to slightly higher returns over time.

Regular Mutual Funds: You invest through an intermediary. The AMC pays them a commission out of your investment, resulting in a higher expense ratio and potentially slightly lower returns compared to the direct plan of the same fund.
